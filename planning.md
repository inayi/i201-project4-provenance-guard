# Provenance Guard — Planning (Milestones 1 & 2)

Provenance Guard is a backend any creative-sharing platform can plug into to judge
whether a piece of text-based creative work was written by a human or generated by an
AI, to score confidence honestly, to surface a plain-language transparency label, and
to give creators a fair path to appeal. The system is built around one non-negotiable
principle: **on a writing platform, falsely accusing a human author of using AI is
catastrophic**, so every design decision below biases toward protecting the human.

---

### 1.3 Narrative journey of a piece of creative writing

A poet named Maya finishes a short prose poem and pastes it into a platform that uses
Provenance Guard. The platform sends `POST /submit` with `{"text": "<Maya's poem>"}`.

1. **Rate limiter.** The request is keyed by Maya's creator id (falling back to IP).
   Because she has submitted only a handful of pieces this minute, she is well under
   the 10/minute ceiling and the raw text passes through untouched.
2. **Signal extraction.** The detection engine hands the *same* text to three
   independent judges. **Burstiness** measures how much her sentence lengths vary —
   Maya writes with a jagged rhythm (a two-word line, then a long winding one), so the
   coefficient of variation is high and this signal reports a low AI probability.
   **Repetition** measures vocabulary diversity, repeated sentence openers, and
   duplicate bigrams — Maya's language is fresh and varied, so this too reports low.
   **LLM Judge** ships up to 6,000 characters of her text to Groq's
   `llama-3.3-70b-versatile`, which reads for idiosyncrasy and stylistic texture and
   returns a JSON verdict; it also reports a low AI probability. If Groq were
   unreachable or returned malformed JSON, this signal would *abstain at 0.0* rather
   than guess — a deliberate choice so a broken model can never inflate suspicion.
3. **Ensemble fusion.** The three floats are combined as
   `p_ai = 0.50·llm + 0.25·burstiness + 0.25·repetition`, yielding a single number
   near 0.10 for Maya.
4. **Label mapping.** Because `p_ai < 0.40`, the engine selects the **`likely_human`**
   attribution and its verbatim transparency label.
5. **Audit log.** Every raw score, the fused `p_ai`, the attribution, the confidence, a
   text snippet, and an ISO-8601 timestamp are written as one row to the SQLite
   `audit_log`, which mints a UUID `content_id`.
6. **Response.** Maya's platform receives a standardized JSON payload with the
   attribution, `confidence`, `final_p_ai`, the transparency label text, and the full
   per-signal breakdown, and renders a quiet "Likely human-written" badge next to her poem.

Weeks later a different creator, Sam, submits a heavily templated piece that scores
`p_ai = 0.91` and is labeled **`likely_ai`**. Sam disagrees. His platform calls
`POST /appeal` with the `content_id` and his reasoning. The database looks up the
original row, sets its `status` to `"under_review"`, stores Sam's reasoning
alongside the untouched original decision, and returns confirmation. A human moderator
later queries the open-appeal queue, sees Sam's reasoning next to the original scores,
and makes the final human call — exactly the human-in-the-loop escape hatch the design
guarantees.

## 2. Detection Signals & Calibration

Three **independent** signals each emit an AI-likeness probability in `[0.0, 1.0]`.
Independence is the point: each can be fooled in isolation, so we require corroboration.

### Signal 1 — Burstiness (sentence-length coefficient of variation)
- **Mechanism.** Split text into sentences, take each sentence's word count, then
  compute `CV = std_dev / mean` of those counts. Low CV → uniform sentence lengths →
  higher AI probability; high CV → jagged human rhythm → lower AI probability. The raw
  CV is mapped to a probability via `ai_p = clamp(1 − CV/0.9)`.
- **Why it differentiates.** Human prose is bursty — writers instinctively mix terse
  fragments with long, clause-heavy sentences. Default LLM output tends toward an even,
  metronomic cadence, producing a conspicuously low CV.
- **Blind spots.** Uniform-by-design forms fool it: a haiku, a list poem, or a
  structured stanza has intentionally regular line lengths and will read as "uniform"
  even though a human wrote it. Very short texts (< 2 sentences) give it nothing to
  measure, so it abstains at 0.5.

### Signal 2 — Repetition (composite: TTR + sentence starters + duplicate bigrams)
- **Components.**
  - **Type-Token Ratio (TTR):** `unique_words / total_words`. Low TTR = recycled
    vocabulary; below ~0.55 it starts contributing to the AI score.
  - **Repeated sentence starters:** the most-common first-two-words opener as a
    fraction of sentences. LLMs reuse scaffolding like "First… / Second… / Third…".
  - **Duplicate bigrams:** `1 − (unique_bigrams / total_bigrams)`. High duplication
    signals formulaic phrasing.
  - Combined: `0.45·diversity_penalty + 0.35·opener_repeat + 0.20·bigram_repeat`.
- **Why it flags AI.** It catches *template scaffolding* — the structural monotony of
  machine text — which burstiness alone can miss when sentence *lengths* happen to vary.
- **Blind spots.** Legitimately repetitive human art trips it: rhythmic refrains,
  villanelles, choruses, incantatory prose, and rhetorical anaphora ("I have a
  dream…") are deliberately repetitive and can score high despite being human.

### Signal 3 — Groq LLM Judge (`llama-3.3-70b-versatile`)
- **Prompting strategy.** A single deterministic (`temperature = 0`) call instructs the
  model to act as an AI-text detector and weigh phrasing, idiosyncrasy, factual
  texture, and stylistic uniformity, then output *only* JSON.
- **JSON output contract.** `{"ai_probability": <float 0..1>, "reason": "<one short
  sentence>"}`, requested via Groq's `response_format={"type": "json_object"}`. Parsing
  happens in an isolated try/except.
- **Restrictive fallback.** On a missing API key, a network/API error, or JSON that
  cannot be parsed, the signal logs loudly and returns **0.0** — never 0.5. Defaulting
  low guarantees a degraded LLM can only pull `p_ai` *toward human*, never manufacture
  a false accusation.
- **Blind spots.** Hyper-polished human writing — a peer-reviewed academic abstract, a
  heavily copy-edited corporate blog — can read as "AI-like" to the model and inflate
  the score. Conversely, an LLM explicitly prompted to write messily can slip under it.

### Calibration & Fusion
The three scores are fused with fixed, documented weights:

```
p_ai = (0.50 × llm_score) + (0.25 × burstiness_score) + (0.25 × repetition_score)
```

The LLM is the strongest single judge, so it carries half the weight; the two cheap
statistical signals split the remainder and act as a **corroborating counterweight**.
This matters because of the **asymmetric risk**: falsely accusing a human of using AI
is catastrophic (it impugns their integrity and can cost them their reputation),
whereas missing some AI content is a tolerable, correctable miss. The
`p_ai ≥ 0.80` "Likely AI" threshold is deliberately high, which means the LLM signal
**cannot single-handedly** cross it: even a maximal `llm_score = 1.0` contributes only
0.50, so a "Likely AI" verdict *requires* the statistical signals to agree. Corroboration
is mandatory before the system will ever point a finger.

## 3. Uncertainty Representation & Thresholds

The system treats the score as a **spectrum of confidence, never a binary verdict.**

- A **`p_ai = 0.55`** means "the signals are genuinely conflicted." Perhaps the LLM
  leaned AI-ward but burstiness and repetition disagreed. The system refuses to force a
  call and lands the piece in **Uncertain** — it says "we don't know" out loud rather
  than guessing and risking a false accusation.
- A **`p_ai = 0.95`** means "all three signals strongly and independently agree this is
  machine-generated." Only at this level of corroboration does the system make a
  confident **Likely AI** statement.

Precise numeric cutoffs:

| Range | Classification | Meaning |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| `p_ai < 0.40` | **Likely Human** (high-confidence human) | No strong signs of AI; the default, protective outcome. |
| `0.40 ≤ p_ai < 0.80` | **Uncertain** (borderline) | Signals conflict or are weak; the system withholds judgment. |
| `p_ai ≥ 0.80` | **Likely AI** (high-confidence AI) | Strong, corroborated evidence across signals. |

**Different user experiences on the frontend.** These bands are meant to render as
three visually distinct states, not shades of one warning:
- *Likely Human* → a quiet, reassuring badge (or nothing at all) — the work is
  presented on its own merits with no friction.
- *Uncertain* → a neutral, honest note that authorship couldn't be determined; no
  accusatory styling, no scary iconography — it normalizes "we're not sure."
- *Likely AI* → a clear, calm disclosure label that **always** advertises the right to
  appeal, so the accusation is never a dead end for the creator.

## 4. Transparency Label Design

Verbatim, final user-facing text for all three variants — plain, empathetic, and
non-technical:

| Variant | User-facing label text |
|---------|------------------------|
| **High-confidence AI** | 🤖 Likely AI-generated. Our analysis suggests this content was probably produced with the help of an AI tool. This is an automated estimate, not a certainty. The creator can appeal this label. |
| **High-confidence Human** | ✍️ Likely human-written. Our analysis found no strong signs of AI generation. This is an automated estimate, not a guarantee of authorship. |
| **Uncertain** | ❔ Authorship uncertain. Our analysis could not confidently determine whether this content was written by a human or generated by AI. We are showing this honestly rather than guessing. Treat the work on its own merits. |

Design intent: every label states it is an *estimate*, not a verdict; the AI label
explicitly offers a path to appeal; and the uncertain label frames "we don't know" as
an honest, non-punitive outcome rather than a soft accusation.

## 5. Appeals Workflow

### Creator payload (data contract)
`POST /appeal` accepts JSON:

```json
{
  "content_id": "<uuid of the original decision>",
  "creator_reasoning": "<free-text creator reasoning explaining why the classification is wrong>"
}
```

Both fields are required; a missing/empty field returns `400`, and an unknown
`content_id` returns `404`.

### Tying the appeal to the original record + state mutation
The database handler (`register_appeal(content_id, reasoning)`):
1. `SELECT content_id FROM audit_log WHERE content_id = ?` — if no row exists, returns
   `None` → API responds `404`.
2. On a hit, issues `UPDATE audit_log SET status = 'under_review',
   appeal_reasoning = ? WHERE content_id = ?`. Crucially, this mutates **only** `status`
   and `appeal_reasoning` — the original scores, `final_p_ai`, and `attribution` are
   **never overwritten**, preserving the integrity of the audit trail.
3. Returns the full updated row so the API can echo the original decision back to the
   creator alongside the confirmation.

### Moderator view of the open-appeal queue
A human moderator querying open appeals
(`SELECT * FROM audit_log WHERE status = 'under_review'`) sees a typed row like:

| content_id | timestamp | raw_text_snippet | final_p_ai | attribution | status | appeal_reasoning |
|------------|-----------|------------------|-----------|-------------|--------|------------------|
| `b95730af…` | `2026-07-01T04:13:17Z` | "The system is good. The system is fast…" | `0.9606` | `likely_ai` | `under_review` | "I wrote this myself from personal experience. I am a non-native English speaker…" |

The moderator sees the original machine decision *and* the human's counter-argument
side by side, and makes the final human ruling. Automated re-classification is
intentionally out of scope — the appeal exists precisely to route contested cases to a
person.

## 6. Anticipated Edge Cases & Safety Infrastructure

### Two stress-test content edge cases
1. **A structured human poem (haiku / villanelle / list poem).** Deliberately uniform
   line lengths spike the *burstiness* AI score, and a repeating refrain spikes the
   *repetition* AI score. Two of three statistical signals fire on a genuinely human
   work — the exact false-positive scenario the design fears. This is why the LLM
   carries 50% weight (it can recognize the piece as human creative writing) and why
   the 0.80 threshold demands broad agreement before accusing.
2. **A hyper-polished human academic essay.** Clean, formal, low-idiosyncrasy prose can
   push the *LLM judge* AI-ward, while burstiness may also read as uniform. Here the
   repetition signal (high vocabulary diversity, no template scaffolding) is the
   dissenting voice that keeps `p_ai` out of the "Likely AI" band — again, corroboration
   is the safety valve.

### Rate limiting — `POST /submit`
- **Rule:** **10 requests / minute** per creator (keyed by `X-Creator-Id`, falling back
  to remote IP), plus a **200 / hour** global backstop across all endpoints. Appeals are
  separately capped at 5 / minute.
- **Justification.** Real creative-writing submission is a slow, deliberate act: a
  writer finishes a piece, submits it, and waits for the result — realistically a
  handful of submissions in a burst while revising, not a sustained stream. 10/minute
  sits comfortably above that human ceiling while being far below what an adversary
  needs. The two threats are (a) **token-exhaustion / cost DoS** — each submission
  triggers a paid Groq call, so an unbounded endpoint is a direct financial attack
  surface — and (b) **decision-boundary scraping** — an attacker probing thousands of
  crafted inputs to reverse-engineer and evade the detector. Minute-windows kill bursts;
  the hourly cap blunts sustained abuse; both leave genuine creators completely
  unobstructed. Exceeding a limit returns a clear `429` JSON payload.

### SQLite `audit_log` schema
Every attribution decision and every appeal is captured in one canonical, append-mostly
table (appeals mutate only `status` + `appeal_reasoning`). This is the base schema; the
stretch features add three more columns (`content_type`, `signals_json`,
`verified_creator`) and a separate `creators` table — see §7.

```sql
CREATE TABLE IF NOT EXISTS audit_log (
    content_id        TEXT PRIMARY KEY,       -- UUID minted per submission
    creator_id        TEXT NOT NULL,          -- submitting creator (from request body)
    content_type      TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'text',   -- 'text' | 'image_metadata'  (§7)
    timestamp         TEXT NOT NULL,          -- ISO-8601 UTC of the decision
    raw_text_snippet  TEXT NOT NULL,          -- first ~200 chars of submitted text / metadata summary
    burstiness_score  REAL,                   -- Signal 1 raw AI probability (NULL for image rows)
    repetition_score  REAL,                   -- Signal 2 raw AI probability (NULL for image rows)
    llm_score         REAL,                   -- Signal 3 raw AI probability (NULL for image rows; 0.0 if abstained)
    signals_json      TEXT NOT NULL,          -- full per-signal breakdown for any modality (§7)
    final_p_ai        REAL NOT NULL,          -- fused ensemble score
    attribution       TEXT NOT NULL,          -- 'likely_ai' | 'uncertain' | 'likely_human'
    confidence        REAL NOT NULL,          -- max(p_ai, 1 - p_ai): certainty in the stated direction
    verified_creator  INTEGER NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,     -- 1 if creator held a certificate at decision time (§7)
    status            TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'classified',   -- 'classified' | 'under_review'
    appeal_reasoning  TEXT                    -- creator reasoning, NULL until appealed
);
```

This schema is the auditable source of truth: it retains **all raw signal scores** (not
just the fused result) so any decision can be re-derived and defended, a timestamp for
chronology, the confidence and attribution shown to the user, and the appeal metadata
needed to drive the moderator queue.

## 7. Stretch Features

All four stretch features are implemented. This section is written *before* building
each, per the project requirement.

### 7.1 Ensemble detection (≥3 signals, documented weighting) — DONE
Covered above: burstiness + repetition + LLM judge fused `0.25 / 0.25 / 0.50`, with a
corroboration-gated AI threshold so no single signal can trigger a false accusation.

### 7.2 Analytics dashboard — `GET /analytics`
A read-only aggregation over the `audit_log` table exposing detection patterns and
platform health, computed on demand (no extra storage):
- **attribution distribution** — counts of `likely_ai` / `uncertain` / `likely_human`.
- **appeal rate** — appeals filed ÷ total submissions (the additional metric of choice,
  because a rising appeal rate is the earliest signal that the detector is misfiring).
- **average confidence** and **uncertain rate** — how often the system withholds
  judgment, a direct read on calibration.
- **per-signal averages** — mean burstiness / repetition / LLM scores, to spot a signal
  that has drifted or is systematically dominating.

### 7.3 Provenance certificate ("Verified Human" credential)
A creator can earn a durable credential through an explicit verification step, separate
from any single classification:
- **Earning it:** `POST /verify` with `{creator_id, attestation}`. The creator signs a
  human-authorship attestation (a non-trivial free-text oath). On success the system
  mints a certificate (UUID + timestamp) and stores it in a `creators` table.
- **Displaying it:** once verified, every `/submit` response from that creator carries a
  `provenance_certificate` object and the transparency label is prefixed with a
  **"✅ Verified Human Creator"** badge. The `verified_creator` flag is also snapshotted
  into the audit log row, so the credential's state at decision time is auditable.
- **Semantics:** the certificate is a *trust signal about the creator*, not an override
  of the AI verdict — a verified creator's obviously-AI text can still be flagged. It
  raises the human context a reader sees, in line with the project's attribution goal.

### 7.4 Multi-modal support — a second content type
`POST /submit` accepts `content_type: "text"` (default) or `"image_metadata"`. The image
modality runs an **independent signal set** over structured metadata rather than prose:
- **generation-software tag** — metadata `software`/`tool` naming a known generator
  (Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, DALL·E, Firefly, Imagen, Flux) → strong AI signal.
- **generation prompt present** — a `prompt` field is a near-certain AI tell.
- **missing camera EXIF** — no `camera_make`/`camera_model` where a real photo would
  carry them → moderate AI signal.
- **C2PA / provenance manifest** — an explicit capture vs. AI-generation claim pulls the
  score toward human or AI respectively.

These fuse (software 0.40, prompt 0.30, missing-EXIF 0.15, C2PA 0.15) into the *same*
`p_ai`, and reuse the *same* thresholds, attribution codes, and transparency labels as
the text pipeline — so the confidence semantics and label UX are identical across
modalities. Image decisions are logged with `content_type = 'image_metadata'` and their
modality-specific signal breakdown stored in `signals_json`.

### Schema additions for stretch features
The `audit_log` gains `content_type`, `signals_json` (per-signal detail for any
modality), and `verified_creator`; the three text-signal columns become nullable (NULL
for image rows). A new `creators` table holds the certificate registry:

```sql
CREATE TABLE IF NOT EXISTS creators (
    creator_id      TEXT PRIMARY KEY,
    certificate_id  TEXT NOT NULL,   -- UUID minted on verification
    verified_at     TEXT NOT NULL,   -- ISO-8601 UTC
    attestation     TEXT NOT NULL    -- the creator's signed human-authorship oath
);
```

## AI Tool Plan

This section identifies, up front, where AI code-generation tools will be used and
exactly what parts of this planning document will be fed to them as input, so the
generated code can be verified against the spec rather than trusted blindly.

### Milestone 4 — second signal + confidence scoring
- **What the AI will generate:** (1) the second detection signal (the stylometric
  *repetition* function), and (2) the confidence-scoring / ensemble-fusion logic that
  combines the signals into a single `p_ai` and maps it to a label category.
- **Inputs provided to the AI:** §2 (Detection Signals & Calibration — the fusion
  formula `p_ai = 0.50·llm + 0.25·burstiness + 0.25·repetition` and per-signal
  definitions), §3 (Uncertainty Representation & Thresholds — the exact `≥0.80 /
  0.40–0.80 / <0.40` cutoffs), and the §1 Architecture diagram (so the function
  signatures fit the pipeline's data flow).
- **Verification step:** confirm the generated scorer uses the *asymmetric* thresholds
  from §3 — AI tools tend to implement symmetric cutoffs around 0.5, which would silently
  break the false-positive protection. Any divergence is corrected before wiring in.

### Milestone 5 — production layer (labels + appeals)
- **What the AI will generate:** (1) the label-generation function mapping a score to the
  correct transparency-label text, and (2) the `POST /appeal` endpoint.
- **Inputs provided to the AI:** §4 (Transparency Label Design — the verbatim text of all
  three variants), §5 (Appeals Workflow — the data contract, state mutation, and moderator
  view), and the §1.2 Appeal-flow diagram.
- **Verification step:** ask the AI to emit all three label variants and confirm the text
  matches §4 exactly; confirm the appeal handler mutates only `status` +
  `appeal_reasoning` and never overwrites the original decision.

### Milestone 6 / stretch — image-metadata pipeline
- **What the AI will generate:** the `evaluate_image_metadata()` signal set for the
  multi-modal stretch feature.
- **Inputs provided to the AI:** §7.4 (the image signal list and weights) and the §1
  Architecture diagram (to reuse the same threshold/label mapping as text).
- **Verification step:** confirm image decisions reuse the *same* thresholds and label
  text as the text pipeline, so confidence semantics stay identical across modalities.
